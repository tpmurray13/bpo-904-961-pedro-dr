Market Context

Fayetteville's multifamily market is anchored by Fort Bragg — one of the largest U.S. military installations — which creates a deep, counter-cyclical tenant base of active-duty personnel, veterans, and military families. The submarket's 8.9% vacancy is elevated relative to national norms, reflecting new supply deliveries over the past 24 months, but absorption trends are positive at 49 units in the trailing 12 months across the submarket.

Tally Villas' current vacancy of 6.2% is better than both the competitor and submarket averages, demonstrating stable demand for the product type despite the subject's below-market rent position. The rent gap of $195/unit to the comp set average and $351/unit to the submarket average represents significant upside for a new owner with a disciplined renovation and re-leasing strategy. Even modest improvement to $1.00/SF effective rent ($1,088/unit) would add approximately $185,000/year in gross revenue, translating to $120,000+ in additional NOI and approximately $1.5–$1.8M in value at current cap rates.

Grant-Murray Real Estate — BPO Narrative

Tally Villas is a well-located, 81-unit market-rate apartment community in Fayetteville's Woodfield neighborhood, situated approximately 2 miles from Fort Bragg — one of the world's largest military installations. The property's 1997 vintage, controlled access, uniform 2BR/1.5BA floor plans, and walk-up townhouse-style layout provide a stable operational profile with proven occupancy demand.

The trailing 12-month income statement (April 2025 – March 2026) reflects a NOI of $368,641 ($4,551/unit). Notably, Cleaning & Maintenance ($130,357) and Insurance ($63,770) ran above typical market norms during this period; however, the actual reported NOI is used as the basis for this opinion without adjustment, presenting the property on its as-reported performance.

Based on the actual trailing 12-month NOI of $368,641 and a market-supported cap rate of 6.50%, Grant-Murray Real Estate's Broker Price Opinion for Tally Villas is $5,671,397 ($70,017/unit), with a supportable range of $5,266,297 to $5,898,252 (7.00% to 6.25% cap rates on actual TTM income). This opinion is grounded in as-reported performance and does not underwrite any expense normalization — what the property has actually produced is the basis for value.

The most compelling investment thesis for Tally Villas is the rent gap: the subject's effective rent of $903/unit is 18% below the 15-comp competitor average of $1,104/unit and 28% below the submarket average of $1,260/unit. A disciplined re-leasing program targeting $1.00/SF ($1,088/unit) would add approximately $185,000 in annual gross revenue and $120,000+ in NOI, producing $1.5–$1.8M in additional value. This upside is not reflected in the current income but provides meaningful optionality for a value-add buyer.
